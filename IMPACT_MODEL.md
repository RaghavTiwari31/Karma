# KARMA: Enterprise Impact & ROI Model

KARMA's goal is to convert **passive reporting** into **proactive execution**. By intercepting waste *before* money moves or contracts renew, KARMA fundamentally shifts enterprise cost accountability.

---

## 📉 The Baseline Scenario

Consider a median mid-size enterprise or fast-scaling startup:

- **Total Annual Software/Cloud Spend:** ₹5,00,00,000 (₹5 Cr)
- **SaaS Waste Baseline (Industry Average):** ~30%
- **KARMA's Target Reachable Waste:** 15% – 20%
- **Target Gross Annual Savings:** ₹75,00,000 – ₹1,00,00,000 (₹75L – ₹1 Cr)

---

## 🧮 Back-of-the-Envelope Math (Per Pillar)

KARMA captures savings across three distinct waste pillars. Here is the breakdown of how the ₹75L–₹1Cr target is realized annually.

### Pillar 1: Interception (Ghost Approver)
*Target: ₹35,00,000 (₹35L) / year*

When employees request new software or add seats to an existing contract, approvers rarely have the context to reject them. Ghost Approver checks real-time utilization and benchmark rates at the exact moment of the request.

**The Math:**
- 20 requests per month at an average value of ₹5,00,000.
- Ghost Approver intercepts 100% of these.
- In 30% of cases, KARMA identifies overlapping tools or suggests reducing the requested seat count by 20% based on actual historical rollout velocity.
- Calculation: `20 requests × 12 months = 240 requests`
- `240 requests × 30% intercepted = 72 optimized requests`
- `72 optimized requests × ₹50,000 avg. savings = ₹36,00,000`

### Pillar 2: Proactive Pruning (Waste Calendar)
*Target: ₹30,00,000 (₹30L) / year*

SaaS auto-renewals frequently trap companies. Contracts ride out their shelf life because teams miss cancellation windows or fail to aggregate utilization data in time for negotiation. 

**The Math:**
- Enterprise holds 50 key vendor contracts, averaging ₹10,00,000 each.
- Annual renewal event for every contract.
- The Waste Calendar flags 10 contracts (20%) yielding under 60% active ROI ninety days before their renewal date.
- With 90 days to act, procurement downgrades tiers, downsizes licenses, or churns completely.
- Calculation: `10 flagged contracts × ₹3,00,000 avg. downgrade/cancellation value = ₹30,00,000`

### Pillar 3: SLA Enforcement (SLA Monitor)
*Target: ₹10,00,000 (₹10L) / year*

Vendors routinely breach uptime agreements, but enterprises fail to claim the corresponding service credits because the monitoring loop is disconnected from procurement.

**The Math:**
- Enterprise pays ₹2,00,00,000 (₹2 Cr) across critical cloud, hosting, and API vendors.
- Statistically, 2–3 significant SLA breaches occur annually across the vendor portfolio (e.g., 99.5% uptime drops to 98%).
- Standard contract penalties require explicit claims within 30 days for 5–10% of the contract value.
- The SLA Monitor tracks this dynamically and autonomously files/flags the claim penalty for procurement.
- Calculation: `2 breached contracts (Avg value ₹50,00,000) × 10% penalty clause = ₹10,00,000 in recovered credits`
